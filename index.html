For those that have eyes to see, let them see

- Mary Magdalene

Cosmic Harmonium invites us to recognize the world of form, identity, and narrative as Māyā (the material world)— not unreal, but not what it seems to be. By shining light (our conciousness) into this present moment to see what is real. Eckhart Tolle wrote in The Power Now- true life is found in couscious presence, not in mental constructs of past or future. As our dear friend Ilona Ciunaite once said the mind is hijacking the experience. Heaven, Nirvanna, Shambhala all represent the same state of being, which is recgonizing the present moment as the divine perfect that it is. For those that have eyes to see, let them see. Artists often describe their creative process as a form of communion with something greater than themselves. Artist Javier Loredo’s “The being within” series portrays the human body as a luminous vessel of consciousness, inviting all that are welling to see, to look within and recognize the light, love, and life as who we are. Similarly, Laura Pupo’s work explores the relationship between the inner world of the soul and natures external world(Maya), she shows us how the external world mirrors the internal world. As above so below, as within so without. We (all beings) act as vessels for universal energies to create through us. This perspective sees the artist as a conduit through which the collective spirit expresses itself. As Larry Culliford notes, “Art forms a priceless living bridge between the everyday psychology of our minds and the universal spirit of humanity” In today's interconnected world, the internet serves as a powerful platform to share spiritually infused art. Websites and online galleries allow artists to reach audiences across the globe, fostering a sense of unity and shared experience. This digital accessibility aligns with your intention to make art available to everyone, reflecting the universal nature of creativity. If you're interested in exploring more about spiritual art or connecting with like-minded people, feel free to ask. I'm here to assist you on your creative journey.
