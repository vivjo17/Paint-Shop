The purpose of Cosmic Harmonium

Cosmic Harmonium invites us to recognize the world of form, identity, and narrative as Māyā (the material world)— not unreal, but not what it seems to be. By shining light (our conciousness) into this present moment and see what is real. Eckhart Tolle wrote in The Power Now- true life is found in couscious presence, not in mental constructs of past or future. As our dear friend Ilona Ciunaite once said the mind is hijacking the experience. Heaven, Nirvanna, Shambhala all represent the same state of being, which is recgonizing the present moment as the divine perfect that it is. For those that have eyes to see, let them see.

Art: a Spiritual Extension of the Self

Artists often describe their creative process as a form of communion with something greater than themselves. Artist Javier Loredo’s “The being within” series portrays the human body as a luminous vessel of consciousness, inviting all that are welling to see, to look within and recognize the light, love, and life as who we are. Similarly, Laura Pupo’s work explores the relationship between the inner world of the soul and natures external world(Maya), she shows us how the external world mirrors the internal world. As above so below, as within so without.

How can I see the Perfection of this everlasting Moment?

First by silencing all thought over and over again, until it becomes natural to you again. I say again because this is our natural state of being. We were born internally silent and now we are becoming aware and moving back to our natural state of internal silence. Life is not lived through thinking it is lived through experiencing/feeling.

Art as a Collective Creation

We (all beings) act as vessels for universal energies to create through us. This perspective sees the artist as a conduit through which the collective spirit expresses itself. As Larry Culliford notes, “Art forms a priceless living bridge between the everyday psychology of our minds and the universal spirit of humanity”
